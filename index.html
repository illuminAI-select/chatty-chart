How to Use Chatty Chart

What is Chatty Chart?

Chatty Chart is a powerful visualization tool that reveals the hidden value in your unanalyzed communication data. Most organizations analyze only a tiny fraction of their communications—this tool helps you see exactly what you're missing.

The iceberg visualization shows:

  • Black squares = Unanalyzed data (what you're missing)
  • Transparent squares = Analyzed data (what you can see)

Getting Started

  1. Review the defaults: The tool starts with typical business communication patterns (45% calls, 25% email, etc.)
  2. Customize to your reality: Adjust the sliders to match your actual communication breakdown
  3. Set what's analyzed: Update the "Currently Analyzed" percentages to reflect what you can actually see today
  4. Watch the visualization: The grid updates automatically to show your data coverage gap

Channel Controls

Each communication channel has two key settings:

  • % of Total Communications: How much of your total data volume comes from this channel
  • Currently Analyzed: What percentage of this channel's data you can actually see and analyze

Tips:

  • Use the +/− buttons for quick 5% adjustments
  • Click the lock icon to prevent a channel from being rebalanced
  • Toggle the switch to enable/disable entire channels
  • Click the ▼ arrow to collapse/expand sections

Quick Actions

  • Analytics Coverage Scenarios (10%-100%): Instantly set all channels to show what X% total coverage would look like
  • Auto-Balance Channels: Automatically distribute percentages across unlocked channels to total 100%
  • Update Visualization: Force a new random distribution of squares in the grid
  • Reset to Original Values: Restore all settings to defaults

Important Notes

  • Total channel percentages must equal 100%
  • If you get an error, use Auto-Balance Channels to fix distribution
  • The visualization uses 10,000 squares (100×100 grid) for precision
  • Changes update automatically with a short delay to prevent lag

Use Cases

Chatty Chart is perfect for:

  • Sales presentations demonstrating the analytics gap
  • Client discovery meetings
  • ROI demonstrations for analytics investments
  • Stakeholder education about data blind spots
